# Human-in-the-Loop Checkpoints

Use these checkpoints when designing or reviewing an AI-enabled workflow.

## 1. Input quality

- Who is responsible for preparing inputs to the AI step?
- What checks are needed before data or prompts are sent?
- How are edge cases or missing data handled?

## 2. AI output review

- Who reviews AI outputs before they move forward?
- What criteria define an “acceptable” output?
- When should the reviewer reject, edit, or rerun the output?

## 3. Escalation paths

- What happens if the AI output is unclear, contradictory, or risky?
- Who can the reviewer escalate to for help?
- Is there a documented path for pausing or bypassing the AI step?

## 4. Logging and traceability

- Are prompts and outputs logged for important decisions?
- Can someone reconstruct why a decision was made?
- How long are these records kept?

## 5. Continuous improvement

- How will issues and failure modes be captured?
- Who decides when to update prompts or workflows?
- How are improvements communicated back to the team?

## 6. Human override

- Is it clear that humans can always override AI recommendations?
- Are there situations where AI should never be used?
- Are those situations documented in the workflow?
