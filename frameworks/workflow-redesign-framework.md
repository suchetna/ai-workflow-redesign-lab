# Workflow Redesign Framework

This framework helps teams redesign workflows so AI improves execution, decision-making, and handoffs rather than becoming an extra layer of complexity.

## 1. Define the current workflow

Questions:
- What is the workflow trying to achieve?
- Who owns each step?
- What tools are used today?
- Where are delays, bottlenecks, or repeated effort showing up?

Strong signals:
- The workflow is clearly mapped
- Ownership is visible
- Bottlenecks are specific, not vague

## 2. Identify AI opportunities

Questions:
- Which steps are repetitive?
- Which steps depend on summarization, classification, generation, or retrieval?
- Which steps still require human judgment?
- Where could AI improve speed or quality without reducing accountability?

Strong signals:
- AI is applied to narrow, useful steps
- Human judgment is preserved where needed
- The opportunity is tied to a real workflow issue

## 3. Redesign the workflow

Questions:
- What changes when AI is introduced?
- What steps are removed, combined, or improved?
- Where are new review points needed?
- How do handoffs change?

Strong signals:
- AI simplifies the workflow
- New steps are justified
- Roles and handoffs remain clear

## 4. Add human review

Questions:
- Which outputs must be reviewed by a human?
- What counts as an acceptable output?
- Who signs off before work moves forward?
- What happens when the AI output is wrong or unclear?

Strong signals:
- Human review is explicit
- Escalation is possible
- Quality expectations are clear

## 5. Support adoption

Questions:
- Do teams know how to use the redesigned workflow?
- Are examples and guidance available?
- Is the workflow easy to repeat?

Strong signals:
- The workflow can be taught
- Good practice is shareable
- The redesign is practical, not theoretical
